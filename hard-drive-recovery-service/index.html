When To Choose A Hard Drive Recovery Service Over Software

December 19th, 2010 by GuestPoster

Calling upon a hard drive recovery service is something which thankfully doesn’t occur too often. In this post, we’ll look at some of the tools you can use to attempt the repairs for yourself if necessary, or retrieving the data, before resorting to external services.

You could begin by trying to do a hard drive repair of software faults for yourself using Microsoft’s disk checking utility [Windows Explorer/pick a partition/properties/tools] in order to analyze the drive for any signs of bad sectors. If the application cannot repair the faults then it well cordon off these sectors so that they are not used again at a later stage.

There are several commercial tools that are widely available (e.g. HDD Regenerator) which will work out better than the Microsoft tool at fixing boot sector issues, boot record problems, sector damage, etc. you should also check with your hard drive/computer manufacturer to see if there are any hard drive repair tools/management software that they provide (these may also be free to customers).

If all you need to do is recover the data on the disk then your next option is to resort to data recovery software. You could select one of the many good freeware options (e.g. Recuva or Smart Recover) which will work on most Windows hard drives and are capable of finding lost data, deleted files and even data on a formatted disk. The commercial options will be more capable of finding a greater quantity of files in adverse circumstances (e.g. after formatting a disk where the file allocation table is cleared out).

A hard drive recovery service will use much more expensive versions of these types of tools in order to attempt a repair or recovery of your data. In particular, they will be capable of finding data on your disk if there has been a mechanical fault with the disk (e.g. seized components, damaged disk platter etc.) using a variety of hardware reading devices.
