Laptops Are Better Than Tablets

March 29th, 2011 by GuestPoster

Its seems every where you go you see a new advertisement for a the newest tablet. Whether its the iPad, the Playbook, the Galaxy, or any of the others there is simply no point in owning one. Everything a tablet can do a laptop can do better. The only thing that is driving this trend is that Apple created a “cool” product with iPad and all the Apple lovers out there had to buy one and now every other company is trying to cash in on the craze.

Now sure, tablets have some nice features, but not enough to merit having an entire device just for those features. Takes its slimness for example. While it is fantastic that the manufacturers can fit all the gadgets inside and keep it that thin is that really reason enough to buy one? No. You can find many of the laptops under 300 dollars are getting close to dimensions of a tablet so get one of those instead. It is a real computer, just small and slim.

The touch screen is another cool feature that tablets are maximizing. But once again, this technology is not exclusive to tablets either. There have been industrial computers that have used touch screen interfaces for years. There have even been Tablet PC laptops, where the screen folds down flat and can then be used like a tablet, but has the key features that make laptops superior. Keyboard, more memory, and faster processor. There are many laptops under 500 dollars that have this capability, in fact right now Dell is selling the Inspiron Duo which is what they are calling a convertible laptop because of the way the screen flips over lays flat against the keyboard.

It all comes down to the fact that tablets are just underpowered, tiny, touch screen laptops. If that is what you want why don’t you spend even less money than a new iPad costs and get a real computer instead of an expensive toy?
