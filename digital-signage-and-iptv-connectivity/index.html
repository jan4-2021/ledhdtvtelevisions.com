Digital Signage and IPTV Connectivity

November 28th, 2010 by GuestPoster

Enterprise digital signage software is not new, but represents a new way for people who do no know how to advertise using digital methods. When it comes to a mall, a restaurant, a bus station, a school (high school or even university), digital signs can deliver a meaningful message to those who may be waiting to be served. If you are an owner or operator of a network of LCD displays whose content can be controlled from a central server, then you probably manage digital signage. A corporate closed-circuit television network for internal communications can be very beneficial and necessary when it comes time to deliver a message in a timely way. When you need to do this you will need to make sure that you are using a network with the right hardware and infrastructure.

The most beneficial feature of the connectivity options for digital sign software is that it gives the option of altering the message according the needs and wants of the customers, all done remotely. Whether you connect via VGA or HDMI to you display does not matter. The thing that matters most in this situation, generally has to do with your connection to the network or the internet. This connection can be done in a number of ways including cat5 or cat6 Ethernet cabling or even cellular cards using a cellular phone tower. In some rare instances it could be okay to connect to a wireless network.
