Comparison Of Computer Backup Options

February 22nd, 2011 by GuestPoster

The two major computer backup options used most widely these days are making local backup copies to HDD or uploading files onto an online backup service. This post looks at the pros and cons of each to identify what types of users they will suit.

The traditional way of doing a laptop data backup has always been to burn a CD copy of critical data. High capacity external hard drives now mean you can simply synchronize the data (i.e. cloning files between the laptop and hard drive memory). The benefit of this is that you have the flexibility of choosing which files and folders to backup and have faster access to these files especially if you need to restore system files.

Depending on the size hard drive, you could also even do an full laptop data backup or ghost image to complete restore your system if it has a fatal failure.

The major problem for many users however would be forgetting to backup. You have to manually run the synchronization software as and when backups are needed and this can be forgotten.

Commercial online backup servers are a recent phenomenon. They provide client applications which can synchronize the uploading of files to their backup servers, the cost. Users who travel a lot like this type of approach as you do not need to be turning around additional hardware which your laptop. You could also access the data via a web interface on any PC if desired.

The real drawback to these online computer backup options are the data limits. Depending on your tariff and subscription costs for these services you will be limited to maybe one or two gigabytes of space on their servers. This is not sufficient for doing a full backup so you can really only consider these systems for partial backup copies of critical data.
