Many Advantages Of LED Landscape Lighting

January 23rd, 2011 by

Outdoor LED landscape lighting has been increasing in popularity for years.Â  Fortunately, the supply chain begins to provide its own incentives as a product becomes popular. Initially, distribution channels are established, and outlets wait to determine whether supply can satisfy demand.Â  Once production and supply levels become consistent and meet demand, then competitive pricing begins to occur.

Competitive pricing establishes additional incentives for the buyer. Consequently, buyers are increasingly converted to product lines that both meet their needs and their budget. Some people are unaware that LED landscape lighting now fits into this category, and is the opposite of price-prohibitive.

LED stands for Light Emitting Diode. This is a fancy way of saying that the light source is a semiconductor. Most people are familiar with LEDs used as indicator lights. They have now been developed into sources that provide a variety of lighting strengths and densities, from very dim to exceedingly bright. Regardless of the amount of output, one tremendous advantage of LED over incandescent is the substantially reduced energy consumption.

LEDs also tend to be smaller, more durable, and more reliable. They last longer and switch faster. Understanding all of these unique differentiating characteristics helps a consumer to recognize the better comparative value of LEDs. Clearly, consumer interest has increased. As a result, LED landscape lighting fixtures have begun to skyrocket in popularity. A wide variety of types and styles are now available to suit any home exterior.

Low voltage LED landscape lighting provides an effective outdoor solution for many reasons. An LED is considered solid state. This means that heating a filament is not the way that light is produced for this device. Rather, electricity moves through a chemical compound. Exciting this compound is the process that creates light.

Consider the following upsides. Low wattage LED units emit light for more than 60,000 hours each. When used properly, each can last for an average of 10-15 years. You may actually move before these bulbs need to be replaced.Green considerations are a factor as well. Energy consumption can be reduced by 30% or more. LEDs use no mercury, and reduce carbon dioxide output.

LED landscape lighting kits generate little to no heat, and are a safe alternative to other exterior fixtures. They are resilient with frequent switching, as well as extreme temperature changes and fluctuations. A variety of colors and shades are available to coordinate with all architecture and landscaping.When it comes to outdoor lighting, no other solution is more advantageous than LEDs. This landscape lighting is friendly to the budget, friendly to the environment, and safe. No better value currently exists.
