LG 47LW550 3D LED TV Review

June 10th, 2011 by admin

You would be forgiven to dismiss the LG 47LW550 3D LED TV as just another 3D TV like many others in a long line of recently released models. However it is worth a second look, because the LG LW550 has one big difference from the majority of other 3D TVs because it uses passive 3D TV rather than active 3D.

Other than the fact that it includes seven pairs of the lightweight 3D glasses that you get in cinemas, rather than the cumbersome active shutter 3D glasses that most other models use, how does this TV stand up to itâs active 3D TV rivals?

The quality of high definition pictures is good on the LG LW550, but not outstanding. Colors are vibrant with good definition, a small amount of motion judder can be detected. For standard definition viewing, as you would expect the picture is less defined. As a whole the LG 47LW550 has good, solid but unspectacular HD performance and is not as good as the best LED TVs out there, but then this is reflected in the competitive price tag.

However, 3D performance is where this LG 3D TV excels. The inherent characteristics of this passive 3D TV means that it produces wonderfully colorful and bright images with virtually no crosstalk (the ghosting of images due to the left and right eyeâs images over-lapping). Also watching this 3D TV is refreshingly easy because the passive 3D glasses are comfortably light and there is no fatigue associated with the flickering of active shutter 3D glasses. The reduced resolution compared to the full HD 3D of active 3D models would be noticeable if you were to compare them side-by-side, but otherwise the 3D performance of this LG 3D LED TV is impressive.

The Smart TV abilities are impressive and have been considerably expanded upon compared to last years models. Like Samsung models the LG Smart TV includes a large range of smart apps, a web browsing and home networking, however it would be fair to say that when using the LG smart TV features it is not as easy to find your way around the menus as it is with Samsungâs implementation, and the web browser is as equally clunky also.

The selection of connectors is adequate, with one downside being that Wi-Fi connectivity is not inbuilt (an optional adaptor must be purchased), but it does have an ethernet port. There are two USB ports for connecting to digital media on memory sticks etc, however unlike some competitorâs models it can not be used to record live TV.

Sadly like most slim LED TVs in this class the sound quality of the LG LW550 is a disappointment. The speakers are not substantial enough, however some of the innovative sound related feature such as Clear Voice II help to make up for this.

In summary this LG 47LW550 3D LED TV review is a big thumbs up especially for itâs 3D performance and price which compared to its active 3D TV rivals makes it a bargain and a sure fire hit with consumers.
