Panasonic Viera U50 Plasma TV Review

Those looking for an affordable television that still packs a considerable picture-quality punch may want to consider a model from the Panasonic TC-U50 line. With excellent black levels, solid color reproduction, and strong off-angle viewing, the performance of this television is great for casual entertainment areas and family rooms; a lack of features for the line may make it a secondary choice for serious gaming or videophile enthusiasts.

For more information, check out my entire Panasonic U50 TV review below.

Models available in the TC-U50 line include:

Panasonic TC-P50U50 -50 inches
Panasonic TC-P60U50 – 60 inches

Picture Quality, Black Levels, and Color

Black levels are a huge strength of the TC-U50; the line competes in this area with all but the highest-end plasma televisions. The screen also delivers good shadow detail and excellent brightness, even when the screen is completely white or filled with bright colors. Color reproduction is superb and is without the blue cast in darker hues that you often see in LED and lower-end plasma screens. Although the television performs admirably, it doesn’t quite reach the level of the higher-end Panasonic or Samsung plasmas across the board. Differences can be noted in bright viewing and processing, for example.

Pansonic U50Â Design

With the price point on the TC-U50, you probably aren’t expecting a work of art, which is good. What you get is a product that doesn’t try to be anything other than a solid flat-screen television. With a mostly black case and thin silver accent strip, the TC-U50 is low profile and won’t detract from the rest of your room.

The pedestal is pretty standard and doesn’t have a swivel option. The remote control follows the television’s lead and offers a basic and functional design that is friendly to any user.

Connectivity

The TC-U50 has a single USB port, an SD card slot, and a component/composite input for video. Unlike most of its peers, the television only has two HDMI jacks, making it inconvenient for families who want to use it as an all-purpose entertainment center with functionality for gaming, movie viewing, and watching television. You can side step this issue with a secondary HDMI switch, which costs less than the difference in price between the TC-U50 and some other televisions.

Video Processing

There are some minor video processing issues with the TC-U50 that don’t show up in more expensive models. A few scenes in certain movies may have a choppy look, but this is minor and won’t impact daily entertainment values.

Power Consumption

Energy costs are always a concern with plasmas, and the TC-U50 doesn’t bring any improvement. Consumption is on par with other plasmas of similar size, although there is a lower energy setting that means the 50 inch TC-U50 qualifies for Energy Star certification. Experts estimate that the TC-P50U50 will cost about $45 over the course of a year for average users.
