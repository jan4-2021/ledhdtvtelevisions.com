The Versatililty Of TV/DVD/VCR Combos

May 25th, 2010 No Comments

Many people who enjoy watching home movies have accumulated a collection of both DVDs and video cassettes which they have movies on. In order to make use of these older videos which are still in your video cassettes, you need to have a VHS player. However though, these players are already long considered obsolete and are things of the past. Fortunately, many manufacturers of electronic appliances have recognized this dilemma and have created a very useful product – the TV DVD VCR Combo. Basically, these are just an all-in-one type of television units where they allow you to use a DVD and a VHS tape.Â  They have proven to be very useful and convenient especially when you need to do some recording from your old VHS cassette tapes to a DVD.

These sets come with all the same functions and features you would find on a standard DVD or a VHS player. You have the options of fast forwarding, pausing, go to the next track, rewinding the tape, and etc. You also have features for use with your video discs which are standard to DVD only players. Using a combo unit not only allows you to easily switch between video and disc movies, it also saves time and space by being one convenient built-in unit. The smaller models are also handy to have if you plan a trip to visit family and want to share some older video tapped home movies with them.

One very good thing about these combo units is that they have become more and more appealing these days. Manufacturers have been competing to capture a big slice of the market. Because of this, a wide range of high definition TV DVD VCR combo units have been proliferating across many home appliance store. Another variant, the TV DVD Combo, is now even available with LED screens with highly vivid images. You will find that these combo units offers the same sharp image and high definition graphics just like a standard HD television unit has.

Prices of these versatile combo units can vary between $200 up to a whooping $2,200 depending on the screen size, screen type, and built-in features. Popular brands like Philips, LG, Sharp, and Sony have been dominating the market because of their affordable models.
