Panasonic GT20 Plasma TV Review

By admin on June 19, 2011 in Panasonic Plasma TV Reviews, Plasma HD TV, Plasma TV Reviews

It is for good reason that many Panasonic G20 Plasma TV reviews have rated the G20 as best the best in its class. Using the new breed of Panasonic Neo plasma displays the release of the Panasonic P42G20 and P50G20 was eagerly awaited and did not disappoint.

The G20 is the non 3D TV version of the more expensive VT20. It might not be 3D capable, and it does not have the most aesthetically pleasing design, but it produces stunning results and is packed with some great technical features whilst retaining a more than affordable price tag.

As already mentioned the G20 uses the latest generation of NeoPlasma technology, which uses improved phosphors which operate faster and produce better colors. Combined with Panasonics 600Hz technology and an updated video engine (V-Real Pro) all round video performance has been enhanced from previous generations of plasma TVs. In practical terms this means stunning colors, smoother motion handling and improved contrast ratios. The most noticeable improvement is with the contrast, in particular the depth of blackness produced within images whilst still retaining fine detail. Also the color reproduction is ultra realistic providing natural and vivid images. Both color and contrast is consistent over the full display area, and no negative effects are experienced from a wide viewing angle, unlike with many LCD and LED TV models which utilize back lighting.

The G20 has 4 HDMI, a DNLA ethernet port, 2 USB ports, SD media card slot and Wi-Fi using an optional USB adaptor. Using VieraCast the G20 can connect to internet services such as YouTube, Facebook and Skype. VieraCast does not provide as extensive web services as others brands do (namely LG, Samsung and Sony), but it is well implemented an easy to use and is sure to improve as more internet services become available through Panasonics internet app store.

Along with the ability to stream your home media using the ethernet port and the SD slot using all the commonly used media formats, the Panasonic G20 can also record live TV broadcasts using an external hard disk. This enables the ability to pause, rewind and playback TV broadcasts.

Audio abilities of the Panasonic G20 are good and better than most of the other models in its class, however it still could be better at lower frequencies but in general it produces well balanced and realistic audio effects.

General usage of the G20 is a pleasant experience, with well implemented menus and controls. For the energy conscious consumers amongst you, the G20 also favours far better than the average plasma TV when it comes to energy consumption. The G20 does this because of the qualities if the improved NeoPlasma displays along with the help of some clever technology such as ambient light detection which dims the display in dark viewing conditions, plus screen saver and auto standby mode which kicks in after a set period of inactivity.
