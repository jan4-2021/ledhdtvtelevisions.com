LCD , Plasma, LED and 3D TV News

By admin on May 4, 2011 in 3D TV, Full HD TV, LED TV, Plasma HD TV

The last few years have seen impressive growth in the High Definition TV market. There have been many new developments which have fuelled this increase, including the introduction of LED TV and 3D TV and ever increasing competition in the existing technologies Plasma TV and LCD TV. Technology and value for money has improved and the availability of HDTV broadcasts has widened all contributing to the demand for HDTV.

It is inevitable that we are going to see a slowdown in demand at some point and this has been confirmed by recent forecasts from the industry and manufacturers them selves. 2010 saw almost 250 million new units shipped an increase of 18%. It is predicted that in 2011 this increase will drop to 12%, this is still however somewhat impressive in the middle of a global recession.

It is expected that the emerging market in the ânon-western worldâ will see greater increase in demand for HDTVs than the worldwide average as they catch up with the established markets of the US and Western Europe which are expected to level out over the next 3 years along with Japan. These trends are largely reflective of the replacement of CRT TVs with flat panel LCD and Plasmas. The established markets in the US, Europe and the Far East have now largely adopted this technology whilst emerging nations are still on the uptake.

LCD TVs still continue to clearly be the market leader and are predicted to retain more than an 80% market share of HD TVs sold worldwide in 2011. Of this figure, LED TVs will makeup about 50% of total LCD TVs sold, (not forgetting that despite the name LED TVs are still an LCD TV just with LED lighting rather than fluorescent).

The big success story of the later half of 2010 and fist half of 2011 is of course the rise of 3D TV. The uptake of 3D is expected to account for up to a third of TVs sold in 2011 and grow even further in subsequent years as all the major manufacturers incorporate it as a standard feature in their range of LCD, Plasma and LED TVs.

A surprising figure in amongst all of this is that Plasma TVs actually saw a 30% growth in 2010 compared to the previous year. This can be attributed to the fact that plasma TV prices have dropped whilst still maintaining a good reputation for quality. In the past plasma TVs were considered at the higher end of the market in price and quality of picture. Now prices are much more affordable plasma TV has gained popularity.
