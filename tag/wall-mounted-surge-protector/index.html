Protect Your Flat Screen TV With A Wall Mounted Surge Protector

No comments

The wall mounted surge protector is a staple for owners of flat-screen TVs, speaker systems, laptops, or any other electronic equipment for one simple reason: the first power surge that comes along will wipe out any data saved and, in all likelihood, destroy the device itself. What do we mean by a “power surge”? Each electronic device has been manufactured to handle a particular voltage. For example, a standard laptop computer can only handle a few hundred volts of electricity, while a typical lightning strike can deliver up to 63,000 volts of electricity in a few seconds. “Transient voltage,” as when lightning strikes the power grid that is directly connected to your electronic device, can do considerable damage to whatever is plugged into the power outlet in your wall.

That’s why wall surge protectors have been on the market for so long. An in wall surge protector takes up very little space and, if your device is plugged into a wall mount surge protector rather than directly into the power outlet, then you can rest assured that your device is safe, along with any data you have stored on it.

What makes this simple device so effective against power surges? Each one contains an electronic component called a “variable resistor” that directs the excess voltage away from circuitry that is sensitive to the level of voltage present. In short, these “variable resistors” or “varistors,” as they are commonly known in the industry, protect your valuable devices by absorbing the “shock” effect of lightning and other transient voltage spikes.
