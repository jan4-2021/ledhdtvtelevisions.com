Panasonic Viera GT50 Series Plasma TV Review

The Panasonic TC PGT50 series features four plasma televisions that range in screen size from 50 to 65 inches. The specifications are the same for all sizes and Panasonic’s characteristic plasma performance is excellent with this series. Detailed shadows, deep blacks, and great-off angle viewing provide an enjoyable experience for the movie lover, gamer, or casual television viewer. For more information about the Panasonic TC PGT50 series, read my complete review below.

Available Models

The TC PGT50 is available in four size options, as follows:

Panasonic TC-P50GT50 – 50 inches
Panasonic TC-P55GT50 – 55 inches
Panasonic TC-P60GT50 – 60 inches
Panasonic TC-P65GT50 – 65 inches

Picture Quality

Out-of-the-box, picture quality may not appear as sharp as Panasonic’s ST50 lineup or Samsung’s flagship PNE8000. A few minutes of calibration will bring the picture quality of the PGT50 to the same level as these competition televisions, which is to say that the image is beautiful and very high quality. Before tweaking with calibration, the THX setting on the PGT50 gives it a boost in shadow quality, but it is dimmer than the other televisions. Given that calibration brings the ST50 line up to par with the PGT50, consumers may opt for the less expensive ST50 television based solely on picture.

Design

The Panasonic TC PGT50 is edged with an industrial-chic metallic silver that provides a clean, modern style. The silver contrasts nicely with the black bezel edge. The television sits atop Panasonic’s standard pedestal, which some consumers and reviewers note is a bit lackluster. Overall, the television will make an attractive and sleek addition to any home theater or entertainment space. At the same time, the television is classy enough that it will not detract from living room dÃ©cor.

The remote for the PGT50 features large buttons with plenty of backlighting, so you can easily control the television even in dim lighting. New to this series is a glossy face on the remote. Although this makes the piece look modern, it may make it hard to keep clean as the face will show fingerprints and smudges.

As with other models, Panasonic keeps its standard user-interface, which features a yellow text, blue background, and very little in the way of graphical bells and whistles. The simple presentation of the interface makes it seem a bit basic, but all functional elements are present. There is even a help screen that offers basic user manual information at the touch of a button.

3D Television Capability

For the most part, the Panasonic TC PGT50 series performs similarly to the ST50 series when it comes to 3D viewing. Cross talk was barely present, and perhaps only the Samsung PNE8000 provides a better quality image. You do need to calibrate the PGT50 for proper 3D performance, and some experts recommend that you change the 24p Direct In setting to 48Hz. The television defaults to 60Hz for this setting. Enjoyment of 3D films does require a good quality, well-fitting set of glasses, which Panasonic offers.

Bright Viewing

Image quality in a bright room is always a concern with plasma screens. Like other Panasonic models, the PGT50 provides fair performance in a bright room, maintaining black levels and limited reflection. The GT50 is not quite as good as competing Samsung models in this area, although the screen should be adequate for casual daytime viewing in a bright room.

Smart TV

Panasonic’s VieraConnect Smart TV interface provides an easy-to-use layout that allows users to move easily between functions. The interface provides a function similar to tabbed browsing where users can easily access recent functionality, making it faster to move between different functions. Options include video applications like Netflix, numerous music apps like Shoutcast and Pandora, and social networking access that allows you to use Facebook and other accounts on screen. Numerous apps are available through the Panasonic store, and you can even find free apps to download.

Connectivity and Power Consumption

The PGT50 comes with numerous inputs. Four HDMI inputs provide for all your video needs and a PC input allows you to use your television as a mega-monitor. These inputs are on the back of the television. A breakout cable is provided to allow for component and composite connections.

As with any plasma screen, the Panasonic TC PGT50 uses more electricity than completing LED models. Consumers can reduce utility costs by dimming the screen, but that does impact viewing quality slightly.

Summary

Overall, the Panasonic TC PGT50 delivers outstanding plasma screen performance without any surprises. Sticker price on this series runs between $ 1,200 and $2,500, depending on the size of the screen and where you make the purchase.
