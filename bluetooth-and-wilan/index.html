Bluetooth and WiLan

May 1, 2011

There are two wireless standards today that are getting a lot of press, and are hard to avoid in your Electronics Superstore. They are WiLAN and Bluetooth, and seem to be everywhere. What are these two standards and what are they used for?

WiLAN is used to describe the wireless technologies in IEEE 802.11 standard. The 802.11 standard has evolved over the years, and now there are several sub-standards like 802.11b, 802.11g and 802.11n (there are others). For the most part, the newer stuff is backward compatible with the older stuff – although the new offers much more throughput. This standard, administered by the IEEE Standards Committee, is a series of wireless technical specifications adopted by industry to create a common platform for wireless devices. Common WiLAN enabled devices would include routers, hard disk drives, newer printers, wireless internet cameras, and a lot of other stuff that can benefit from a wireless medium-range interface. Note that WiLAN normally works best in a relatively small (a few hundred feet) range.

Bluetooth is another one of those words you’ll hear a lot of. Bluetooth is different from WiLAN, although it could fit under the more general term of WiFi. Bluetooth is administered by a trade association – the Bluetooth SIG (special interest group), which is a group of companies that have formed this SIG in a not-for-profit, proprietary way. Any device that wants to call itself “Bluetooth Compliant” must have the blessing of this SIG – which means it must meet the technical and marketing standards. Bluetooth is considered a PAN – Personal Area Network – and it creates a very small (a few inches to a few feet) wireless network centered around a “master device”. The master can be “paired” to up to six other devices, called “slaves” (they can trade this master ability between them). The pairing process ensures that only authorized devices are talking to each other. Think of Bluetooth as a wireless replacement for a cable, and you’ll get a good idea as to the product breadth. Devices like Bluetooth for computers, which could be paired with a keyboard or a mouse, or headsets paired with a cell phone, are common devices you’ll see in the market.

The market for both these technologies is huge, so keep your eyes open at that same superstore – you’ll now know what they’re selling!
