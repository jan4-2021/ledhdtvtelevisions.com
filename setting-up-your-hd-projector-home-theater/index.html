Setting up Your HD Projector Home Theater

March 3rd, 2010 by FULLHDTV

Many people that are thinking of putting an hd projector theater in their homes are concerned with how difficult it will be to set up the system. However, if certain steps are followed and rules of thumb looked at, having a home theater can be a very real and easy possibility. This article will help you put your home theater together with ease.

The first thing to consider when building a home theater is the actual room that you wish for the theatre to be in. The main thing that should be considered when choosing the room is the size of theater screen you wish to have in the room because this will determine which room you can use for your hd projector home theater. The easiest way to decide how much room you need to decide how much screen you want. Every foot of the width of the screen you need to have two feet of distance from the wall. Something else to consider with the room you are using is the color of the walls because this can affect the image projected. A dark colored room with blackout blinds is the ideal situation.

The next thing to think about is the type of screen you want. There are many different options for screens such as a white wall or even a sheet, but for the best viewing results, an actual screen is ideal. Screen can come in ones that are mounted on the wall or can even be drop down, but searching for the right size is what you need to focus on.

The next step is just to connect your projector, and all that you need to do is follow the directions on how to connect video connection to the picture source. With the right cables, this process is very easily done especially when following the directions.

Now that your HD projector is hooked up, you can turn on the projector and start the finishing touches. The projector will have to be moved so the image will be aligned with the screen. You can also zoom the image in and out to fit the screen, and then the most important part is to focus the lens. This will make the image crisp and clear to allow for the best viewing possible.
