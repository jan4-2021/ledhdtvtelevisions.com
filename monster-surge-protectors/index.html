Tip For Purchasing A Plasma TV Surge Protector

February 1, 2010

If you’ve already invested in some serious equipment for your home theater, the next must have item after purchasing your system is a plasma TV surge protector for better picture quality as well as for surge protection. Whether you paid hundreds or thousands of dollars, you must protect that investment from harmful power surges. Many different factors cause surges to occur and over time they will ruin an unprotected electrical appliance.

Electrical storms are the number one culprit of surges. Lightening strikes a utility pole or strikes a transformer on a pole and extra current follows the electrical lines into houses and all the electrical pathways found internally. Everyone has heard a horror story of what happens to household appliances when this anomaly occurs. Appliances, wiring and circuit boxes are burned. A power surge also takes place before and after a blackout. In fact, power surges occur daily but are not noticeable or detectable without surge protection, that is, until it is too late.

Every time a larger household appliance like a washer or microwave turns on or off it causes a fluctuation in electrical current. Fluctuations happen when power substations are turned on or off. Besides excess current, partial or current generated that is lower than normal is just as damaging. This is known as a brownout. Over time, these slight variances will damage the electrical components of an appliance. This is why plasma TV surge protectors are of the utmost importance for HD plasma TVs.

A surge protector for plasma TV contains components that regulate voltage. When voltage is in excess of allowable standards, the current is directed to all available outlets and then transferred to the grounding wire, protecting that appliance. Another benefit of surge protectors is a line noise feature. Line noise is generated when more than one appliance is in operation. The static seen on a TV when a vacuum or food processor is running are examples of line noise. While not harmful, it is an irritating inconvenience.

The best surge protector for plasma TV is a matter of choice. The more expensive a unit is doesn’t mean the more protection it provides however you have to keep in mind you get what you pay for. What you want to look for is the Joules rating. Joules ratings are shown for each device. The higher the Joules, the higher amount of voltage levels this wall mounted surge protector can withstand and protect an appliance against. Investing in a plasma TV surge protector is the best investment you can make to protect your plasma television.
