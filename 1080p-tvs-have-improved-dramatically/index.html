1080p TVs Have Improved Dramatically

March 8th, 2010 by GuestPoster

Over the last ten years or so the 1080p televisions have been improved with many different upgrades and advancements. When consumers shop for televisions they no longer shop for the best priced televisions but they are now looking for the best priced most advanced LCD 1080p television they can buy. They want the best quality for the least amount of money. The new and improved HD 1080p televisions are among the most popular being purchased by consumers these days. They are among the best of the high resolution plasma televisions available.

1080p televisions are known for their space-aged technology and unique innovation that was actually developed about 50 years ago. The technology of plasma televisions consists of the use of noble gases trapped behind a thin glass wall. Gases are charged through the process of molecular collisions called photons that create light and is what creates or produces the picture on the television screen. The LCD 1080p television is the clearest and most vivid color television technology manufactured today. HD 1080p televisions are used in hotel lobbies, bars, pubs or sports bars, stock exchanges and at sporting events. The standard size of screen on these televisions is a 50″ wide-screen.

Advances in technology are making the prices of the 1080p televisions drop while providing the consumer clearer more defined pictures to watch, more durable made televisions, and the new
LCD 1080p television is lasting longer. The original HD 1080p televisions had shorter lifespans because of the dissipation of the gases contained within them. The newer model plasma televisions have a longer lifespan of about 60,000 hours. At one time it cost nearly $4000 to buy one of these televisions but they are more common and the prices have dropped to about $2000. The pricing depends on the size of the television as well as the features.
