Generation Y Improves Design Skills through Virtual Reality

By GuestPoster on February 17, 2011 in Other Technology

There are many benefits to exploring the world of virtual reality. It offers state of the art technology for entertainment value as well as a teaching platform for students to learn from. In a recent study, students were given a platform of virtual reality to assist in improving their design skills in a simulation called 2nd Life.Â  This game resulted in successful results to improve their design, problem solving and logical skills.

The application model of 2nd life allowed researchers to create a virtual reality platform by asking students to design a zoo. Students were able to exp lore, design and implement a zoo by using problem solving and logical abilities in a 3D world. Through this experiment, researchers were able to see their design ability progress and come to the conclusion that 2nd generation helped them improve their design skills drastically. It also cemented the notion that the young generation adapts quickly in the virtual world by means of exploring, similar to the video games they have grown up playing.

Since generation Y is a group of people that relate to media, technology and a fast paced virtual environment, it is easy for them to adapt to a 3D model. Teaching students in a virtual world is effective since they have for some time already lived in one, and in this way, it makes learning about design and improving their design skills easier to accomplish than the traditional classroom setting. Online classes can occur wherever their laptop is, and with multimedia outlets such as Facebook, Twitter and YouTube, a younger generation student can improve their design skills by entering a world they are familiar with: the virtual world. Mobile phones allow students to attend class in a coffee shop or their bedroom. They can chat with their professors in chat rooms and interact with their peers online.

When in a 3D environment, students were observed as improving their kinesthetic skills, logical skills and spatial skills.
