The First Commercial Wireless Hard Drives

August 3rd, 2010 by GuestPoster

Seagate has always been one of the leading manufacturers of hard drives and are well known across the world for their premium-grade products. However, their technology has just been taken to the next level with the introduction of the Seagate wireless hard drive. Instead of trying to cram as many terabytes as possible into one unit, this new model fits a smaller amount but is extremely versatile.

The original project is known as D.A.V.E and stands for Digital Audio Video Experience. The case is small enough to hold a 1.8 inch drive inside, that can manage between 10GB to 20GB. Although the capacity is not very high, it is the only wireless drive available on the market. It can connect to your computer or smart phone through either Wi-Fi or Bluetooth, with a fairly impressive transfer speed. A traditional USB port can also be found on the case, in case your computer device does not have wireless output. This technology was designed so you can transfer and access your data while traveling. The interface is made primarily for Bluetooth, although it is still compatible with Wi-Fi.

Seagate external wireless drives can simply be switched on and paired with your phone. A built in battery is include that lasts up to ten hours before it needs to get charged again. Once connected, you computer device can browse just like a normal drive, viewing files and even communicating with nearby Bluetooth devices and transferring information to them. Once the battery has run out, you can charge it simply by pulling it via an adapter into a power outlet or by charging it into a computer. Just like with many other recent gadgets, the primary charging method is made to be done using a computer.
