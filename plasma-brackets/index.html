Plasma Brackets add Viewing Possibilities for Plasma & LCD TVs

January 25th, 2011 by GuestPoster

The cost of Plasma and LCD television monitors has steadily declined which has resulted in an increase in popularity and the possible need for plasma brackets. An investment in a Plasma TV requires a decision about how and where it will be placed in the family or living room, or even bedroom. Some people prefer using a TV stand on which to position their Plasma screens because it often provides extra storage space for DVDs, electrical accessories and entertainment consoles. Others prefer to mount their screens on a wall for optimal viewing pleasure. Those people will invest in wall mounts and plasma brackets to hang the screen from the wall and maximize the monitors’ viewing areas. If properly positioned, a mounted Plasma TV can be well viewed from various positions in a room. There are several types of brackets available on the market so some research may be necessary to determine the best type for an individualâs needs.

The most common mounting bracket on todayâs market is the normal TV bracket. These are designed to horizontally hang the Plasma or LCD screen on the wall which allows for viewing at a better height and enhances the viewing capabilities. Normal brackets are the most economical type of bracket, but tilting brackets increase the viewing range and are now preferred over normal brackets.

Panning and tilting plasma brackets are most common in homes that require the capability of television viewing from specific areas. Tilting brackets allow the monitor to be moved downward or upward to 45 degrees. Panning brackets allow the monitor 180 degrees of horizontal movement.

When shopping for a wall mount or plasma bracket, consumers need to find models compatible with their monitor.
