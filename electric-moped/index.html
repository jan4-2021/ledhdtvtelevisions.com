The Rise of the Electric Moped

December 12th, 2010 by GuestPoster

If you are a person that is very concerned about the environment, or if you’re person that wants to save some money and claim your independence from fossil fuels, then an electric moped may be the way for you to go.Â  Mopeds themselves are not a very new invention, as a matter fact, they been around for quite some time. However, using electricity as a method to power a moped is something that is relatively new to the moped. Electric bicycles have actually been around since the beginning of the 20th century, but they were very large and cumbersome. Now that technology has gotten better with electric transportation not only has the electric bicycle improved, but also the innovation of the electric moped as well.

Electric mopeds are basically electric scooters with pedals on them. An individual can have the option of pedaling their moped if they want to save a little gas or to get started, but usually most people do not use the pedals and they use the electric moped, much like they would electric scooter or electric motorcycle. Most electric mopeds have twist throttles on the handlebars so you can ride it just like a motorcycle by turning the throttle and making the motor work. The more you turn the throttle or twist, the faster the electric moped will go. The electric moped is powered by a motor which is located in the rear wheel of the unit and the motor gets its energy from rechargeable batteries that are located somewhere underneath the seat of the moped.Â  It usually takes about 4 to 6 hours to completely recharge batteries, however, you can still ride about 30 to 40 miles on a single charge, and a moped will usually go somewhere in the vicinity of 20 miles an hour.

Mopeds are great vehicles to ride if you need to go across a college campus or if you are just running errands in a small town. Providing that the weather is really good, and electric moped is a lot of fun to ride around on and get things done. Because of the storage space that is usually built into the average electric moped, there are plenty of places to put things that you buy or things that you need to carry from one place to the next. There are a few minor drawbacks when using electric mopeds. For one thing, you cannot really pedal one for a very long distance because the design of the electric moped is not conducive to long distance pedaling like an electric bicycle would be. Another thing about the electric moped is the fact that you cannot carry somebody extra on the back of your moped like you would a regular gas driven scooter. However, there are many new models coming on the market and they may soon accommodate all of these issues.
Because of the overwhelming interest in electric vehicles (especially electric bikes), you can expect to see improvements made in the area of the electric moped. People all over the world are buying electric bicycles, electric scooters and electric mopeds in record numbers, especially in countries and places where people live very close to where they work. It will be interesting to see the prices of these electric vehicles drop once more manufacturers are servicing an ever-increasing market.
