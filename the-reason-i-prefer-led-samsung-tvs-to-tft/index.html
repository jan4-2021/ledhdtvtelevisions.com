The Reason I Prefer LED Samsung TVs to TFT

by GuestPoster in LED TV


There is some clarification due regarding the title. The expressions TFT and LED are used quite liberally when it comes to TVs and computer displays, but you should know that they’re to not completely different technologies. TFT is a thin film transistor layer to control the LCD panel lying beneath, while LED TVs use the same kind of TFT-LCD combination but the backlight is made of high-power white LEDs. There are other reasons to go LED than just the power consumption and this article aims to list the few most important ones.

Mentioned above, emphasized here; LED variations of TFT based flat-screen televisions consume up to 40% less power than other similar sized TVs. The difference is so significant that it makes up for the slightly higher up-front cost of these TVs, and given that LEDs have an estimated time between failures of 100,000 hours as opposed to the couple thousand hours on CCFL TFTs, it’s easy to see how it will cut a lot of your energy costs during the years to come.

Durability has been covered, but I don’t think it got the amount of emphasis it deserves. An LED TV will operate -in theory- for 50 years if you use it for 6 hours a day, which seems plausible considering that there are LEDs still operating that were made in the 1970′s, when technology wasn’t nearly this sophisticated. If you don’t over-current or heat these electronic elements, they’re unlikely to fail any time in the foreseeable future.

The third, yet still similarly significant factor is image quality, and I don’t think it gets any better than this unless you choose a plasma screen, or wait until OLEDs are made in sizes bigger than the display of a touch-screen mobile phone. The white of LEDs is closer to the optimal color spectrum than that of a CCFL, thus giving you better colors and more contrast ratio, for only a little extra money on top of the ordinary CCFL.

If, however, you want even better contrast (in the millions, rather than the thousands), you want a plasma Samsung TV. If you aren’t concerned about the burn-in effect, which is now largely dealt with through better software, I suggest you go for it. In every other case, forget CCFL-TFT, and get LED based flat-screen TV. It’s possibly the best bang for your buck at the moment.
