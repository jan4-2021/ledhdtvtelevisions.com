Panasonic Plasma TV Review 2011

May 17th, 2011 by admin

Panasonic recently revealed details of their new models of mid and high range Plasmas for 2011. With the entry level range of Panasonic ST30 3D Plasma TV series having already received very positive reviews, the new mid range Panasonic GT30 and top of the range Panasonic VT30 have been eagerly anticipated.

The recent Panasonic GT30 reviews and Panasonic VT30 reviews have been very positive about both ranges with them comparing favourably to both Panasonicâs 2010 equivalent models and also to their current plasma, LCD and LED TV rival models from other manufacturers. The Panasonic GT30 and VT30 showed considerable improvement in contrast levels, motion handling and 3D capabilities. The reason behind this is the new neo-plasma panel from Panasonic and the improved, faster performance of the phosphors responsible for producing the images displayed. The plasma panelâs faster response times have a direct positive effect on the ability to display smoother, finer detailed images and the reduction of 3D crosstalk. Levels of brightness have also been increased, this will be particularly beneficial when viewing in 3D (this is because active shutter 3D TV glasses cause dimming of the images being viewed through them).

Compared to equivalent LED TV sets the Panasonics plasmas compared favourably producing superior detail and motion handling. These qualities are beneficial when viewing 3D images in particular compared the new breed of passive 3D TVs. But then this is to be expected as alternative passive 3D TV is more competitively priced than active 3D TV models. Whilst we are on the subject of the cost of 3D TV, Panasonic has announced that they intend to reduce the price of their active shutter 3D glasses to around $75 per pair. This was inevitable because of the critism manufacturers have received about the price of this essential accessory as well as the fact that Panasonicâs competitors have already implemented similar price reductions.

In line with consumer demand and the general trend towards aesthetically attractive televisions, Panasonic has restyled their 2011 range of plasmas. The redesign has seen the introduction of a thinner bezel with a true flat single glass display panel in line with other leading manufacturers.

Other features to note are the introduction of a âsmooth cinemaâ feature only previously seen in LCD and LED TV models, this adds additional frames in between the 24 frames per second in which movies are shot to produce smoother motion. Also the Panasonic GT30 and VT30 have the ability for auto calibration, a feature that allows users to easily setup all aspects of their plasmas far quicker and more effectively than it could previously be done.
