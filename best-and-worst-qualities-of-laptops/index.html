Best and Worst Qualities of Laptops

By GuestPoster on January 28, 2009 in Computer / Laptop / PC

Laptops have become quite prolific in our society. With everyone from the teenager to the old folks home patron owning their very own laptop. So what is it about laptops that make them so great? What is it that you are sacrificing to carry your computer with you?

The obvious best part quality of the laptop is the portability. Hence the name, lap-top. An entire computer that can fit on the top of your lap. Being able to not just take your files on a thumb drive where ever you want, but the programs that you need are now always available. Price is another benefit, laptops can be incredibly cheap, you can easily find laptops under 200 dollars. A third benefit is that you don’t have to be plugged in to a wall socket to use your laptop, you can be on an air plane or even walking in the park. Netbooks are the newest species evolving from the laptop. They are lighter, smaller, and more portable. They have taken all that is good in a laptop and amplified it.

With all the good that comes with a laptop there are plenty of draw backs that you have to deal with if you are going to carry your entire computer with you. First of all is computer power to price ratio. To get a top of the line processor you are going to have to pay extra to fit it in the tiny laptop package as well as provide the power for the hungry high tech processor. If you are on a budget the majority of laptops under 500 specs are not going to be as impressive as a desktop PC of comparable price. Size, while a positive in some occasions is a negative. If you want to watch a movie you’d much rather watch it on the 24 inch wide screen HD monitor you have on the desk than on the 10 inch netbook.

Both desktops and laptops are good for certain situations, so pick the one you are going to need the most often and go for it.
