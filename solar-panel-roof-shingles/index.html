Solar Panel Roof Shingles Explained

June 14th, 2010 by GuestPoster

If you are wondering how could you save some money on your utility bills, take a look at solar energy. If you install solar shingles for example, you could generate your own electricity and never pay your electric bill. It may be tough if you don’t have money to invest, since installing solar shingles does require an initial investment. But if you have some spare cash and are ready to invest to your future, consider the advantages of solar power.

Don’t worry about aesthetics with your solar shingles, since they will match your regular roof shingles perfectly, since they come in a variety of colors. It allows you to cover only part of your roof with solar shingles and leave the rest with regular roof cover.

Convenience of this type of solar panels does come at a price. You could expect to spend around $5 per watt for such solar setup. It may seem high, but keep in mind that you are paying for convenience — they are much better to install and maintain that traditional solar panels.

After you install solar shingles you will have to make another decision — whether you want to stay connected to the grid or not. If you already have electricity grid hooked up to your house, there is no need to disconnect, since if anything was to happen to your solar system, you still will have electricity in your household.

For remote locations like cabins, cottages and maybe even RV solar panels, off-grid system might be the only option, just be prepared to spend more money on batteries and charge controllers. This setup though provides you with complete independence on public utility grid, which is a huge advantage for some.

If you are considering solar shingles, keep in mind that you are not only saving money, but also helping our planet. Installing solar shingles is a huge step forward in cutting your carbon footprint and taking care of our environment.
