The Best HD Laptop

May 15th, 2010 by GuestPoster

The high definition (HD) viewing experience is no longer limited to the television. Laptop computers now come equipped with crisp, clear displays. However, when it comes to the viewing experience, not all laptops are created equal and finding the best buy laptop with HD requires some comparison-shopping.

The laptop made for high-definition viewing will allow users to watch HD-DVDs, Blu-ray Discs, or both. Blu-Ray offers better overall performance, but it is also much more expensive. Most major motion picture companies offer discs in both formats, so avid movie watchers will not suffer at the hands of either.

Those who prefer to watch Blu-ray Discs should select a Dell, Sony, Acer, or HP laptop. Acer and HP will also meet the needs of HD-DVD viewers, as will Toshiba. That being said, there is a model of each that rises to the top. The Acer Aspire 9810-6829 features a 20.1-inch widescreen and 1680×1050-pixel resolution. The Inspiron 1520 from Dell comes equipped with a 15.4-inch widescreen for Blu-ray viewing. Screen resolution can be changed depending on viewer preference, and the laptop has a MediaDirect button that provides users with one button access to movie controls.

The HP Pavilion dv9500t features a futuristic design and 17-inch widescreen with 1680×1050-pixel display resolution. Those with deep pockets should spring for the Sony Vaio VGN-AR590E for their Blu-ray viewing pleasure. A sharp 1920X1200 pixel resolution and 17-inch screen come standard. At the top of the HD-DVD ranks is the Toshiba Qosmio G45 with its 17-inch TruBrite widescreen and 1900-x1200 pixel resolution and with this quality and expense, you should invest in a good quality laptop bag to protect it.

The best HD laptop is often a matter of choice between those that play Blu-ray discs and HD-DVDs. Dell, Sony, Acer, HP, and Toshiba each offer a great laptop and there is one available to meet every budget. With the great resolution and widescreens standard on most of these computers, who needs a television anymore?
